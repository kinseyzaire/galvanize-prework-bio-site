Artist Kinsey Zaïre, born 1984 in Kansas City, Missouri was home schooled through the third grade and graduated elementary school in a polka dot dress and a mortarboard. Her family then relocated to Franklin, Tennessee where her father built and ran a recording studio, and she dropped out of middle school. In 1997, the family relocated once more to Colorado Springs, Colorado where she dropped out of high school. She moved to New York and studied at Parsons, which she dropped out of. At 21 she moved onto London. A year later she dropped out of London and fell into Denver, Colorado; where she has resided for just over a decade. Her work in all forms bares the purity of deviance and exposes the dignity of wool gathering. Although predominantly a painter she works in all possible media including textiles, ceramics, woodwork, cosmetics, photography and now code.
